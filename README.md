## ğŸ¯ Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Define AI** and describe the major subfields and applications
2. **Formulate real-world problems** as search problems and implement algorithms to solve them (uninformed search, informed search, adversarial search)
3. **Understand the principles** of knowledge representation and logical reasoning
4. **Explain basic concepts** of probability theory for uncertain reasoning
5. **Describe core concepts** of machine learning, including different learning paradigms
6. **Implement and train** basic supervised learning models (linear regression, classification, neural networks)
7. **Recognize societal and ethical implications** of AI technology

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

### ğŸ“š Academic Questions

**Q: Is this course suitable for beginners?**
A: Yes! This course is designed for 3rd-semester students with basic programming knowledge. We start with fundamentals and build up gradually.

**Q: Do I need expensive software or hardware?**  
A: No! Everything uses free, open-source tools. A standard laptop with 4GB+ RAM is sufficient for most exercises.

**Q: How much time should I dedicate per week?**
A: Plan for 6-8 hours per week: 3 hours for lectures/reading, 3-4 hours for exercises and coding, 1-2 hours for review.

**Q: What if I fall behind?**
A: Don't panic! Join study groups, attend office hours, and focus on understanding concepts rather than rushing through material.

### ğŸ’» Technical Questions

**Q: Python vs other languages for AI?**
A: Python is the de facto standard for AI due to its extensive libraries (scikit-learn, TensorFlow, PyTorch) and ease of use.

**Q: Should I memorize algorithms?**
A: Focus on understanding concepts and when to apply them. You can always look up implementation details.

**Q: How important is the math?**
A: Very important for deep understanding! You can use libraries without math, but understanding the foundations makes you a better AI practitioner.

### ğŸš€ Career Questions

**Q: Do I need a PhD for AI careers?**
A: Not necessarily! Many industry roles value practical skills and experience. PhDs are more important for research positions.

**Q: What's the difference between AI, ML, and Data Science?**
A: AI is the broad field, ML is a subset focused on learning from data, and Data Science emphasizes extracting insights from data.

**Q: How do I build a portfolio?**
A: Complete the course projects, contribute to open-source, participate in Kaggle competitions, and build applications that solve real problems.
